# Astrul: Project Rationale
**Team Astrul: S³C Design Competition 2026**

---

## The Vision
Astrul was created to bridge the gap between students and support services at NUST. We wanted to build a portal that feels like a conversation, not a bureaucratic hurdle. By bringing together support requests and academic data on a single surface, we reduce the mental effort required to navigate campus life.

## Problem Solving
Our research showed that students feel disconnected from the support process. They often do not know who is working on their request or how long it will take. Astrul solves this through:
- **Transparency**: The color-coded SLA ring gives a clear visual of the timeline.
- **Communication**: The built-in chat system removes the delay of email threads.
- **Simplicity**: A 4-tap submission process makes asking for help feel natural and fast.

## Design Philosophy
We chose a "Functional Calm" aesthetic. The navy and white theme is a nod to NUST's identity, but with a modern twist. We avoided heavy decorations to ensure the app stays fast and focuses on content. The mobile-first design acknowledges that most students manage their lives through their phones.

## Innovation and Impact
Astrul is not just a UI change. It is a workflow improvement.
- **Bidirectional Persistence**: Every message and status update is saved instantly. This ensures that a staff member can pick up where another left off without losing any context.
- **Cross-Tab Synchronization**: In a world where students have multiple tabs open, keeping data synced across the whole browser is essential for a reliable experience.
- **Mobile Staff Operations**: We optimized the triage view so staff can manage the queue from anywhere on campus, not just from their desks.

## Future Growth
The system is built on a modular architecture. In the future, we can easily add features like automated AI replies for common questions or live location tracking for walk-in services. Astrul is designed to grow with the needs of the NUST community.
